The "Everything Search": Main Library Search Bar

Think of the main search bar on the ACC Library homepage as your supercharged academic search engine. It looks through millions of books, articles, and videos all at once!

Step A

Pick Your Keywords

Don't type full questions. Use 2 or 3 core words. ❌ "Should cell phones be banned in schools?" "cell phones AND school AND distraction"

Step B

Filter Your Results

The filter sidebar is your superpower tool! Use these filter toggles:

  • Peer-Reviewed Journals
  • Date (Last 5 Years)
  • Available Online
Step C

Grab the Goods

Click on an article's title to access the full-text options.

Pro Tip: Click the "Cite" button in the library catalog to get your pre-built MLA citation automatically!

Diving into Specific Databases

The main search bar is helpful, but sometimes it is better to search inside specialized databases. A database is a curated digital closet containing high-quality journals and news articles.

Opposing Viewpoints (Gale)

Best for: Argumentative topics.

Why use it: It provides pre-organized debates featuring pro/con stances side-by-side. It is perfect for constructing solid counterarguments!

Academic Search Complete

Best for: Scholarly articles on various subjects.

Why use it: It contains peer-reviewed research papers reviewed by expert panels before publication. Extremely highly regarded by professors!

Evaluating Google and Wikipedia Results

While databases contain verified content, Google and Wikipedia are where many people begin their research. You can use these platforms, but you must be a detective to confirm their safety and reliability.

1

Check the Domain

Look at the end of the website address (the URL):

.gov / .edu (Safe/Trustworthy)
.org (Double check bias)
.com / .net (Often commercial)
2

Practice "Lateral Reading"

Do not trust a website based on its aesthetic layout alone.

What is Lateral Reading? Open a new tab and search for the author or organization on Google. What are other sources saying about them? If nobody else supports their claims, step away!
3

The Wikipedia Hack

Never cite Wikipedia in your argumentative paper. Instead, use it as a launchpad.

Scroll to the very bottom of the Wikipedia page to the "References" section. Click those links to read and cite the original documents directly!

Source Credibility Assessment Cheat Sheet

High Credibility Sources

• Published peer-reviewed research papers.
• Official .gov files & state statistics databases.
• Established non-partisan journalism outlets.

Warning Sign Sources

• Personal blogs without cited references.
• Sites heavily packed with advertisements.
• Viral social media claims or opinion-only posts.

Quick Check: MLA Reference Formatting (9th Edition)

For ENGL 1302 and SPCH 1315, your sources must be formatted correctly on your works-cited list using MLA guidelines. Here is how a database journal article with a DOI (Digital Object Identifier) is built:

Author Last, First. "Title of the Article." Title of Journal, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. #-#. Name of Database, doi:xx.xxxxxxx.

Make sure to double-check that author names, volume details, and page ranges match your source details precisely!
